The Process

How CED works.

Four elegantly engineered stages. One seamless result.

Step 01 — Water Input

Any water.
No prep needed.

CED's pre-filter stage removes dissolved minerals, chlorine, and particulates before water reaches the electrolysis cell. Tap water, filtered water, or distilled — the device handles it all automatically. A built-in conductivity sensor continuously adjusts electrolysis parameters to compensate for water quality variations.

The inlet filter replaces itself every ~500 cycles. A subtle LED on the device face indicates when a new cartridge is needed — available as a subscription or one-time purchase.

Step 02 — Electrolysis

The split.
Water becomes gas.

At the heart of CED is a Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM) electrolyser cell. When current flows through the cell, water molecules at the anode are oxidised — splitting into oxygen gas, protons (H⁺ ions), and electrons. The protons migrate through the solid polymer membrane to the cathode, where they recombine with electrons to form hydrogen gas (H₂).

CED operates at 1.8–2.2V per cell, carefully controlled by its onboard microcontroller to maximise efficiency while preventing membrane degradation. The process is entirely chemical-free — no reagents, no acids, no catalysts beyond the membrane itself.

Step 03 — Separation

Pure gas.
Oxygen exits. Hydrogen flows.

The PEM membrane acts as a selective barrier. Only protons can pass through it — oxygen produced at the anode is vented safely from the side of the device, while hydrogen collects at the cathode and is channelled to the output port. A micro moisture trap removes any residual water vapour, delivering dry hydrogen gas.

Output purity is ≥99.9% hydrogen by volume. A built-in pressure sensor monitors the output channel and automatically throttles current if back-pressure builds — protecting both the device and your application.

Step 04 — Safety & Control

Smart.
Safe by design.

CED's embedded ARM microcontroller runs continuous diagnostics throughout operation. Temperature, pressure, voltage, current, and gas flow are sampled 100 times per second. If any parameter leaves a safe operating window, the device shuts down in under 10ms — faster than you can blink. The housing is rated IP54 for splash resistance, and the entire device is CE, FCC, and RoHS certified.

Hydrogen is flammable above 4% concentration in air. CED's output flow rate is calibrated to remain below this threshold in standard ventilated spaces, and a catalytic recombiner in the device prevents any residual hydrogen from accumulating internally.

The Science

Built on a century of chemistry.

PEM electrolysis isn't new — it's just never been this small.

⚗️

Proton Exchange Membrane

The PEM is a solid polymer electrolyte — typically Nafion® — that conducts protons (H⁺) while blocking electrons and gases. First developed by General Electric in the 1960s for NASA, it's now the gold standard for compact, high-purity hydrogen production. At just 0.1–0.2mm thick, it's the reason CED can be pocket-sized.

0.15mm membrane thickness
⚡

Faraday's Laws Applied

According to Faraday's second law of electrolysis, 96,485 coulombs of charge produces one mole of hydrogen (2g). CED's cell draws ~9W and produces hydrogen at an efficiency of 65–70% (LHV basis) — comparable to industrial PEM electrolysers, in a fraction of the size. The remaining energy is dissipated as gentle heat, removed by a passive thermal spreader.

65% energy efficiency (LHV)
🔬

Iridium Oxide Catalyst

The anode catalyst — where oxygen evolution happens — uses iridium oxide nanoparticles. Iridium is one of only a handful of materials stable enough to survive the harsh oxidative conditions at the anode. CED uses just 0.3mg/cm² of iridium loading, minimising cost while maintaining performance over thousands of cycles.

0.3mg/cm² iridium loading
🌡️

Thermal Management

PEM cells perform best at 60–80°C. CED's microcontroller uses pulse-width modulation of the cell current to keep temperature in this window. A copper heat spreader transfers warmth to the aluminium housing, which acts as a passive radiator. The result: stable, consistent hydrogen output even after hours of continuous operation.

The Reaction

2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂

2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂

Every water molecule you put in yields two hydrogen molecules out. The oxygen is released safely to the air — the same oxygen that's already in the air around you. Hydrogen is the only output that matters.

Replace costly compressed hydrogen cylinders in research settings. Perfect for gas chromatography carrier gas, hydrogenation reactions, and fuel cell testing at bench scale. Produces on-demand — no storage, no safety permits required for cylinders.

Infuse drinking water with dissolved molecular hydrogen — a growing area of consumer health research. CED produces the hydrogen; you control the infusion. Pure, consistent, and produced fresh each time — no stale canisters, no dissolved metal contamination.

Prototype and test small PEM fuel cells without the complexity of cylinder regulators. CED's regulated 0–0.5 bar output is ideal for micro fuel cell developers who need a clean, consistent hydrogen source at the bench.
